What should I open with tonight's zi char? We're having kung pao prawns and cereal butter crab.
✦ Sommelier

The wok hei smokiness and briny sweetness of cereal butter crab call for wines with enough body to hold their ground — but not so much tannin they overwhelm the prawns.

From your cellar:

  • 🍷 Ridge Monte Bello 2018 — S$185 | Drink 2022–2035 (prime)
    Ridge's flagship Cab blend from Santa Cruz Mountains. The firm acidity and dark fruit cut through the cereal butter richness beautifully.
  • 🍷 Domaine Leflaive Puligny-Montrachet 2020 — S$220 | Drink 2023–2030 (prime)
    Crisp Burgundian Chardonnay — the mineral salinity mirrors the prawn brine and doesn't fight the wok hei.
  • 🍷 Château Pichon Baron 2016 — S$310 | Drink 2024–2040 (prime)
    Second growth Pauillac with cedar and cassis — structured enough for the crab, elegant enough not to overpower.

Worth seeking out:

  • 🍷 Kongsgaard Chardonnay 2021 — ~S$280
    Full-bodied Napa Chardonnay with toasty oak — exceptional with the butter element.
  • 🍷 Sine Qua Non "The Inaugural" Syrah — ~S$450
    Bold, smoky Syrah that echoes the wok hei character — a showstopper for a celebratory zi char spread.

Any dietary notes or budget I should keep in mind for next time? 🍜

Verdict

  • Ridge Monte Bello — structured acidity matches the wok hei smokiness
  • Puligny-Montrachet — mineral salinity pairs with prawn brine
  • Pichon Baron — elegant tannins complement the crab richness
  • Kongsgaard Chardonnay — seek out; buttery weight mirrors the cereal crust
  • Sine Qua Non Syrah — seek out; bold smoke echoes wok hei for impact
